# Rehearsal Protocol — Dry Run Checklist

## Purpose

Systematic practice before live pitch on 21/04. Get muscle memory + identify weak spots.

## Rehearsal schedule

### Day 15/04 evening
- **Rehearsal 1:** solo, reading from script, learn content

### Day 16/04
- **Rehearsal 2–3:** solo, from memory, no script
- **Rehearsal 4:** team mock, team members play judges
- **Rehearsal 5:** record yourself, watch back, note issues

### Day 17/04 (after submission)
- **Rehearsal 6–7:** polish, work on transitions + pacing
- **Rehearsal 8:** dress rehearsal with full setup (laptop, projector test)

### Day 18–20/04
- **Rehearsal 9–12:** maintenance practice, 1–2× per day
- **Rehearsal 13 (night before):** short final run, build confidence, then rest

### Day 21/04 (pitch day)
- **Quick morning warm-up:** 5-min verbal run-through
- **Demo check:** verify video plays, laptop works
- **Breathe. Go.**

## Rehearsal checklist per run

### Before start
- [ ] Timer set (3:00 or 5:00 as specified)
- [ ] Slide deck open and ready
- [ ] Demo video cued
- [ ] Water nearby
- [ ] Note-taker ready (for post-rehearsal review)

### During
- [ ] Time each section
- [ ] Note fumbled transitions
- [ ] Note weak word choices
- [ ] Note eye contact (if in mirror)
- [ ] Note breath control

### After
- [ ] Review timing — under target?
- [ ] What felt weak?
- [ ] What worked well?
- [ ] Update script if needed
- [ ] Rest 10 min before next run

## Top pitfalls + fixes

### "Um" and "uh" fillers
**Problem:** Nervousness creates verbal fillers.
**Fix:** Deliberate pauses instead. Breathing helps.

### Rushing
**Problem:** Trying to fit too much → speed up → lose clarity.
**Fix:** Cut content, not speed. Practice slower than natural pace.

### Monotone
**Problem:** Technical content delivered flatly.
**Fix:** Emphasize numbers, pause after punchlines, vary pitch.

### Losing the audience
**Problem:** Deep technical tangent in first 30 seconds.
**Fix:** Lead with story (anh Minh), not tech.

### Over-rehearsed (robotic)
**Problem:** Memorizing word-for-word → sounds scripted.
**Fix:** Memorize the flow + key phrases, let natural language fill the rest.

### Under-rehearsed
**Problem:** Stumbling, searching for words.
**Fix:** At least 10 rehearsals before pitch day.

## Team mock rehearsal

### Setup
- 1 person pitches
- 2–3 team members play judges (use judge-panel roles)
- 1 person time-keeper
- 1 person note-taker

### Protocol
1. Pitcher delivers full pitch (2:30 video + 30s open + 30s close)
2. "Judges" take 30 sec to write questions
3. 10 minutes Q&A (simulate actual pressure)
4. Team debrief (15 min):
   - What was strong?
   - What felt weak?
   - What would you ask if you were a judge?
   - Adjust for next round

### Role rotation
- Round 1: person A pitches, B+C+D judge
- Round 2: person B pitches
- Round 3: person C pitches (even if A will actually pitch — helps A hear alternatives)

Multiple team members ready to pitch = backup + stronger delivery.

## Recording yourself

Video record at least 1 rehearsal. Watch back:
- **Body language** — stiff? animated? closed-off?
- **Eye contact** — looking at screen or camera?
- **Hand gestures** — purposeful or distracting?
- **Pacing** — rushed sections? slow sections?
- **Volume** — loud enough? varies?
- **Clarity** — intelligible? jargon-heavy?

Self-critique is uncomfortable but invaluable. Most issues become obvious on replay.

## Dress rehearsal (day 17)

Simulate actual conditions:
- **Same outfit** as pitch day
- **Same laptop** you'll bring
- **Test demo** on actual laptop end-to-end
- **Check audio** — speakers loud enough?
- **Check video playback** — no stuttering?
- **Projector/monitor test** — colors look right?
- **Time exactly** as if judges were there

## Day-of protocol (21/04)

### Morning
- Light breakfast (avoid heavy food — affects energy)
- Quick 10-minute verbal run-through
- Avoid caffeine overdose
- Confirm transport to venue

### At venue
- Arrive 45 min early
- Find your slot + check AV setup
- Test your video plays correctly on venue equipment
- Test your laptop connects to projector
- Meet the AV technician (friend, not stranger)
- Breathe

### 5 minutes before
- Quiet moment, deep breaths
- Review key phrases (hook + ask)
- Power pose (stand tall, shoulders back)
- Remember: you know the material

### During pitch
- Start slow
- Breathe between sentences
- Make eye contact with each judge at least once
- If you lose track, pause and check slide (don't panic)
- End strong

### After pitch
- Stay calm during Q&A
- Listen fully before answering
- Don't over-explain — give crisp answers
- Thank judges genuinely
- Exit stage confident

### Post-pitch
- Debrief with team
- Note what questions came up (for future refinement)
- Hydrate + eat
- Wait for results

## Stress management

### If you freeze during pitch
- **Pause, breathe, smile, continue.**
- Judges don't mind pauses, they mind panic.
- You have your slides — look at them for cues.

### If the demo fails
- Transition smoothly: "Let me tell you what this would have shown..."
- Play the video if live fails
- Stay confident — failure handling is itself a signal

### If you get a hostile question
- Stay calm, treat it as curiosity not attack
- Answer honestly — you don't have to agree with premise
- Pivot to evidence if unsupported accusation

### If you don't know an answer
- **"Great question — I don't have a precise number, but here's how I think about it..."**
- Never fake it
- Honesty builds trust

## Post-event

Within 24 hours after pitch:
- Thank you note to each judge (personalized)
- Offer follow-up meeting if appropriate
- Share demo video + deck (if asked)
- Reflect on what to improve for next pitch (there's always a next)
- Celebrate — you earned it either way
